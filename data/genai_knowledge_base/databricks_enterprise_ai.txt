Source basis: Databricks Documentation and course materials.

Enterprise AI means building AI systems that can be used safely inside an organization.
For Generative AI, enterprise systems usually need governance, access control,
monitoring, evaluation, and clear ownership of data and models.

Databricks provides tools for the data and AI lifecycle. MLflow is used to track
experiments, parameters, metrics, prompts, models, and evaluation results.
Unity Catalog is used for governance. It helps teams manage permissions,
discover data and AI assets, and control access to tables, files, features,
models, and functions.

In a GenAI project, governance matters because prompts, retrieved documents,
model outputs, and user feedback can include sensitive information. A team
should control who can read source data, who can deploy models, and who can
view logs. Evaluation should happen before deployment and continue after
deployment.

Agent Bricks is a Databricks approach for creating and optimizing domain-specific
AI agents. The goal is to help teams build agents that use enterprise data,
tools, and evaluation instead of acting like unrestricted chatbots.
